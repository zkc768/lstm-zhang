\section{Validation Results}

We now read the predeclared TCN primary on the frozen validation split. Every
number in this section comes from that split over $n=\numseeds{}$ seeds, unless a
sentence names a different evidence domain. Two seeds yield a descriptive spread
rather than a variance estimate. The readout characterizes the frozen candidate
and ranks nothing: the route declares no final model.

\paragraph{The primary meets the predeclared criteria.}
The predeclared TCN primary reaches \macrofone{} of $0.5170 \pm 0.0009$ across
\numseeds{} seeds, where the $\pm$ value is the standard deviation alone
(Table~\ref{tab:validation}). It exceeds the same-row stratified dummy by
1.69\pp{} in the seed mean, with the smallest per-seed margin at 1.63\pp{}, and it
exceeds the majority-class baseline by 18.8\pp{}. The per-ticker delta is a
positive cross-seed point estimate for all five tickers. The three predeclared
conditions are met. That is a fact about a fixed bar, not a demonstration of
effectiveness: the margin is a thin validation-domain edge above a near-even
floor.

\paragraph{The floor sets the scale.}
The dummy baselines define what ``thin'' means. The same-row stratified floor is
roughly 0.499--0.501 in \macrofone{}, close to the even-class value (the
balanced random prior), while the
majority-class baseline collapses to 0.329 by abandoning one class entirely. The
18.8\pp{} gap over majority therefore reflects the stratified dummy's two-sided
coverage rather than a strong directional signal; the weak-signal reading rests
on the 1.69\pp{} gap over the stratified dummy.

\paragraph{A train-inner control spread, not a family comparison.}
A second evidence domain frames the size of that margin. Train-inner control
comparisons across four control rows, two of them last-step degenerate controls,
show a 0.66\pp{} spread in \macrofone, smaller than the 1.69\pp{}
official-validation margin of the TCN primary over the same-row stratified dummy.
The four rows are train-inner diagnostic controls, not the guarded roster's full
model families, so the 1.69\pp{} margin and the 0.66\pp{} spread sit in separate
domains and form no apples-to-apples pair. The comparison only sets scale; it
supports the evaluation-discipline framing and ranks no model family.

\paragraph{The primary is a TCN, and the fallback never fires.}
The predeclared primary is the TCN configuration on the
\texttt{price\_volume\_time} feature set with window $w=20$. The LightGBM
fallback stays inactive: it is reserved for mechanical or reconstruction
failures, none of which occurred, so no fallback path enters the readout.

\paragraph{The per-ticker edge is positive but uneven.}
The per-ticker deltas are all positive as cross-seed point estimates, yet they
spread widely (Figure~\ref{fig:validation_deltas}, Panel A). CSCO carries the
largest cross-seed mean delta at +2.21\pp{} and JPM the smallest at +1.00\pp{},
and the JPM block-bootstrap interval crosses zero. The pooled 1.69\pp{} margin is
thus an average over a heterogeneous panel, which is one reason the diagnostics in
Section~\ref{sec:diagnostics} ask where the same-row edge concentrates and where
it inverts below the random prior.

\paragraph{How the bands and pooled margins are computed.}
The capped whiskers in Figure~\ref{fig:validation_deltas} are unadjusted 95\%
per-trading-day block-bootstrap intervals conditional on fixed seeds. Whole-day
resampling handles within-day autocorrelation but not between-day regime
clustering or cross-ticker common-factor dependence. The per-group target-row
counts (roughly 30k in Panel A validation tickers and up to 55k in Panel B
guarded periods) are therefore not independent samples, and the bands reflect
scored-row sampling only. The first row in each panel is row-pooled, not averaged: per seed,
pool target rows within the domain, recompute candidate and stratified-dummy
\macrofone{}, take the delta, then average the two seed deltas.
