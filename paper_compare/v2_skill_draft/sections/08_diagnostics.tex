\section{Diagnostics and Robustness}
\label{sec:diagnostics}

The diagnostics put two limits on the readouts. The confidence-based acceptance
signals of a near-base-rate classifier are largely illusory, and the thin edge
inverts across activity terciles.

\paragraph{Calibration error is small, but resolution is near zero.}
On the frozen validation split the pooled probability output has an equal-mass
ten-bin expected calibration error of about $0.010$. The Brier score is $0.2496$,
but its vector decomposition is dominated by the uncertainty term ($0.2499$),
leaving a resolution component near $4.7\times10^{-4}$
\cite{brier1950verification,murphy1973vector}. Small calibration error therefore
does not mean the confidence scores rank cases usefully
\cite{guo2017calibration}, so the ranking has to be tested on its own terms.

\paragraph{Selective prediction barely improves on random ranking.}
On the same split the accuracy-based risk--coverage curve
(Figure~\ref{fig:risk_coverage}) carries no cost model and marks no operating
point. Its area (AURC) is about $0.470$ against an oracle near $0.140$, an excess
area ($e$-AURC) of about $0.330$ in the seed mean. The companion AUGRC is $0.237$
\cite{geifman2017selective,geifman2019selectivenet,elyaniv2010foundations,traub2024overcoming}. These are within-seed,
day-level summaries rather than seed-level estimates over \numseeds{} seeds, and
the gain reads thin. Against the random-ranking reference the predeclared score
lowers AURC only marginally ($\Delta$AURC $=-0.010$; 95\% trading-day
cluster-bootstrap CI $[-0.014,-0.006]$). That is about $3\%$ of the gap between
random ranking and a perfect-ranking oracle (gap-closed 95\% CI $[1.9\%,4.0\%]$).
The gain shrinks further at high coverage. The mean random-minus-score
selective-risk difference over the $80$--$100\%$, $90$--$100\%$, and $95$--$100\%$
bands is only $0.25$, $0.14$, and $0.07$ percentage points.
Because low-confidence rows tend to fall in the high-activity tercile, abstention
removes the very rows where the below-random high-activity limitation is most
visible. Abstention therefore compounds that limitation rather than fixing it
\cite{chalkidis2021trading}.

\paragraph{Component ablation moves little (train-inner domain).}
Before slicing the edge by condition, we check in a separate domain whether it
hinges on which component we credit. One train-inner control precedes the
conditional split. Here \texttt{tcn\_only}
is the primary's TCN and \texttt{dlinear\_only} is the hybrid's multi-scale
DLinear branch (MS-DLinear) with its residual TCN removed. Each control row's gap
to the reference stays within $\pm0.7\pp$ of \macrofone{} (\texttt{tcn\_only}
$+0.03\pp$, \texttt{dlinear\_only} $-0.14\pp$,
\texttt{last\_step\_lightgbm\_control} $-0.12\pp$, \texttt{last\_step\_mlp}
$-0.63\pp$; the last two are last-step degenerate controls). We keep this control
inside its own domain, apart from the validation and guarded readouts. The edge is
insensitive to the control-row choice, so it is not an artifact of which component
we credit, which leaves open where in the data it actually lives.

\paragraph{The edge concentrates in calm bars.}
To find where it lives, we slice the same-row \macrofone{} delta by activity
terciles, the per-(ticker, trading-day) eligible-row-count proxy under the
$3.0$ bps no-trade band, not volume, liquidity, or volatility. The terciles are rank-defined within
ticker, so there is no single global cut point. On the validation split the delta
runs $+5.43\pp$, $+1.91\pp$, and $-1.54\pp$ from low to high activity tercile
(Table~\ref{tab:tercile}). In the high tercile the \macrofone{}
($\approx 0.483$) sits below the even-class prior ($\approx 0.498$) and the
same-row stratified-dummy floor ($\approx 0.500$). The same low-to-high sign
pattern recurs in the guarded 2017--2024 era at $+4.08\pp$, $+0.56\pp$, and
$-2.10\pp$, with a high-tercile \macrofone{} of $0.480$
(Figure~\ref{fig:tercile_map}). The figure's bars are across-seed means with open
circle and diamond seed markers. Its grey sleeves are per-trading-day block
minimum-detectable effects from official validation or measure-only
re-aggregation of frozen guarded dumps, not standard errors or seed-level
uncertainty. This is the only axis we could re-measure on the guarded segment, and
it is a non-independent re-aggregation, not an independent replication. The
magnitude is small, but the low-to-high pattern is same-signed in both windows.
The signal is thus absent where the classifier faces its hardest slice, the most
active bars.

\paragraph{Base-rate and within-day artifacts are excluded; a microstructure one is not.}
A stable but small pattern could be spurious. Three artifact families bear on it:
two are ruled out and one is not. The
near-constant per-tercile dummy floor ($\approx 0.500$) excludes a class-imbalance
artifact, while the up-rate shows only a small monotone gradient ($0.523$, $0.515$,
$0.502$ from low to high, about $2.1\pp$). A fifty-permutation within-day label-shuffle sentinel
excludes within-day leakage. The observed delta exceeds the per-tercile shuffle
maximum in every tercile. In the high tercile it exceeds a more negative shuffle
null, yet still sits below random. Even the below-random value is
therefore not a permutation artifact. The remaining alternative is microstructure. The
calm-bar concentration cannot presently be separated from a bid-ask-bounce
alignment, which is a true row-level feature--label coupling that a within-day
permutation preserves \cite{roll1984simple,lo1990nonsynchronous}, and the
sentinel has no power against it. The discriminating control needs raw five-minute
bars the dump does not carry. We therefore report the map as a diagnostic
boundary on this estimand, not as model-attributable conditional predictability.
If it were model-attributable, it would run against prior intraday-return work
tying short-horizon predictability to intraday timing, active high-volume states,
or seasonal rebalancing
\cite{heston2010intraday,henkel2011timevarying,bogousslavsky2016infrequent}. A
recent regime study reports forecastability in low-financial-uncertainty periods
\cite{ferrer2023forecast}, a regime-level parallel rather than one at our
resolution. We carry the deferred microstructure control as a limitation
(Section~\ref{sec:limitations}).
